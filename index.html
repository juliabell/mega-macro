Key Divergences

Fed Policy: Easing vs. Hawkish Hold
Lyn Alden sees continued gradual easing (rate cuts + balance sheet expansion) as base case. Danny Dayan argues the 175bps of cuts were a policy mistake — the Fed was already below neutral before the 5th supply shock hit. With 6 years above target and inflation expectations unanchored, he sees cutting as dangerous. This remains the single most important divergence for portfolio positioning.
US Dollar: LT Weakness vs. ST Strength
Lyn Alden is structurally bearish on the dollar (multi-year weakening cycle supporting EM). Danny Dayan sees near-term dollar strength from Iran war and terms of trade shock. These views may be reconcilable (ST rally within LT decline), but the timing matters for trades.
S&P 500: Selective vs. Short Bias
Lyn Alden is neutral with selective opportunities (AI capex, banks, EM). Danny Dayan's FCI models point to fading growth impulses and a stagflationary backdrop, leading to a "neutral with bias to short" stance. His CTA models show equities near 2nd sell trigger level.
